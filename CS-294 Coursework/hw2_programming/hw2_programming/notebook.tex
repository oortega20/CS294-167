    \subsection{Remarks before starting}\label{remarks-before-starting}

Please take note of the following few things:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  In your \texttt{hw2\_progrmming} folder, there are two subfolders,
  namely \texttt{four\_point} and \texttt{eight\_point}. In each folder,
  there are four pairs of scenes (8 images in total) and a
  \texttt{numpy} file, which contains the numpy array of coordinate
  correspondences. Each image has dimensions (512, 512). For eight-point
  algorithm, you have 10 points for each pair of scene. For four-point
  algorithm, you have 6 points for each pair of scene.
\item
  coordinate correspondences are given to you as a numpy array, with
  dimensions (4, 2, 10, 2) for eight-point and (4, 2, 6, 2) for
  four-point. The dimensions correspond to
  \texttt{(num\_scenes,\ camera\ angle,\ num\_pts,\ pixel\_coordinates)}.
  I highly encourage you to print out the array to get a better sense of
  the x, y, and z axis.
\item
  To transform pixel coordinates to Euclidean system, you will need the
  intrinsic matrix. In our synthetic dataset, the intrinsic matrix
  \texttt{K} is the same for all scenes and camera angles.
\item
  Some helpful functions are implemented in \texttt{utils.py}. I
  encourage you to take a look before starting.
\end{enumerate}
